# Church Management System PRD

## 1. Summary
The Church Management System will help church staff and ministry leaders manage members, attendance, giving, events, and communications from one maintainable platform.

## 2. Problem Statement
Church staff currently rely on disconnected tools and manual processes to track people, attendance, donations, events, and communications. This leads to duplicated work, inconsistent data, slow follow-up, and limited insight into ministry effectiveness.

## 3. Goals
- Centralize member and household information in one reliable system.
- Make attendance and event participation tracking simple and accurate.
- Support contribution recording and basic stewardship reporting.
- Improve communication with members, visitors, and volunteers.
- Support church divisions such as Sunday School, Youth, and Adult classes as structured groups.
- Track fundraising efforts for the completion of the church building.
- Provide leadership with practical reporting and dashboards.

## 4. Non-Goals
- Building a full accounting system or payroll platform.
- Replacing all existing external communication tools immediately.
- Supporting advanced multi-campus operations in the first release.

## 5. Users
- Church administrators and office staff
- Pastors and ministry leaders
- Volunteer coordinators
- Members and attendees

## 6. Core User Needs
- Find and update person and family records quickly.
- Track attendance for services, classes, and events.
- Record giving and generate simple summaries.
- Manage event registration and reminders.
- Send announcements and follow-ups without manual duplication.

## 7. Functional Requirements
### 7.1 Member Management
- Create, update, and deactivate member and household records.
- Store contact information, family relationships, ministry tags, and notes.
- Search and filter the directory by name, household, status, or tag.
- Assign members to church divisions such as Sunday School, Youth, and Adult classes.

### 7.2 Attendance
- Record attendance for services and events.
- Support simple check-in workflows for in-person events.
- View attendance history and basic trends by person, family, or event.
- Track attendance by division, including Sunday School, Youth, and Adult classes.

### 7.3 Giving
- Record gifts, offerings, and donations by person or household.
- Track contribution history and provide basic summaries.
- Support simple reporting for stewardship and leadership review.
- Track fundraising campaigns and pledges for the completion of the church building.
- Monitor fundraising progress toward specific building goals and deadlines.

### 7.4 Events
- Create events with date, location, and description.
- Track RSVPs and attendance for each event.
- Send reminders or follow-up communications.

### 7.5 Communication
- Send announcements, reminders, and follow-up messages.
- Group people by tag, ministry, or household for targeted communication.
- Track communications sent to a person or household.

### 7.6 Reporting
- Show basic dashboards for attendance, giving, and engagement.
- Export records for reporting or backup.

## 8. Non-Functional Requirements
- Secure handling of personal and financial data.
- Clear access controls for administrators and ministry staff.
- Reliable performance for day-to-day administrative tasks.
- Simple, accessible user experience for non-technical users.
- Maintainable architecture that can grow over time.

## 9. Success Metrics
- Staff spends less time maintaining member and attendance records manually.
- Event and attendance follow-up becomes faster and more consistent.
- Giving records are easier to review and report on.
- Leaders can retrieve practical information without relying on spreadsheets.
